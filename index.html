Your Intro

The first rule in improv is to very simply just say, "Yes" to anything. Which leads right to the second rule, which is to add something, commonly refered to "and". "And" is how we build our reality and everything that we are on stage. YES with these two powerful tools AND a good attitude and willingness to play with others. Any reality is possible.
However just like everything else, to get better at imporv, it takes some instruction and lots of practice to properly use these tools. Once the work is put in, and there is a lot of fun along the way so its hard to call it work, then you get to start performing. There is nothing better than the feeling of joy after a good show.

classes

I started taking classes at The Improv Shop 3 and a half years ago. When I went through the classes there were six levels. These six levels taught us how to preform a long form improv style. Many people are familiar with short form improv, from one of my fav shows "Who's Line is it Anyways". On this show they play a bunch of little short form games. They are set up before hand and then given a suggestion to kick it off.
In long form improv you get a suggestion, and then you make up a play or a sketch. So each scene is completely made up, with only the suggestion to influence everything. Scenes usually run from 1 minute to 10 minutes, though sometimes they can be the whole show, thats called a Monoscene. A typical show has about 9 scenes in them.

Practice

Practice is the best way to build improv skill and to gain team synergy. Most people think practice will be boring, but its like one part structured exercises, then 5 part good old fun improv. However those exercises are very important will help you impove different parts of your improv game. It's comparable to working out. Bicep curls help improv bicep strength. Many of these exercises target certain aspects of improv for improvement.

Most of on stage improv is about trust. The trust you have in each other to support and say yes to any decisions you make on stage. That comes from hanging out and being vulnerable with each other.
